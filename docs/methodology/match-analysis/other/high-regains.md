# High Regains — Methodology

> **Dashboard location:** Match Analysis → Offensive Phase → Chance Creation (High-Regain KPIs, integrated)
> **Analysis type:** Match-level (with a league-table helper)
> **Primary source file(s):** `analytics/high_regains.py` — `detect_high_regains()`, `link_regains_to_shots()`, `compute_high_regain_kpis()`, `analyse_high_regains()`, `build_league_table()`
> **Precomputed parquet(s):** None per match; league table built on demand.
> **Last reviewed:** 2026-06-24

---

## 1 — Purpose

A high regain is winning the ball back in the attacking third. This component detects high regains, links each to any shot that quickly followed, and measures how productive a team's high pressing is — how often regains turn into shots and goals, how much possession value they generate, and where on the pitch they happen. It quantifies the payoff of pressing high, feeding directly into the Chance Creation view.

---

## 2 — Input Data

- **Event types used (`HIGH_REGAIN_TYPES`):** `ball recovery`, `interception`, `tackle`. Tackles require `outcome == 1` (confirmed possession win). Linked shots use `SHOT_TYPE_IDS` / shot names.
- **Qualifiers used:** set-piece qualifier columns (`_SET_PIECE_QUAL_COLS`) to exclude set-piece restarts when `open_play_only=True`; `outcome` for tackles.
- **Coordinate system:** Opta normalised (0–100). High regain requires `x ≥ HIGH_REGAIN_X_MIN = 66.7` (attacking third).
- **Seasons covered:** any match with a CSV; league table aggregates a season.
- **Scope:** per-match (KPIs integrated into Chance Creation) plus a season league-table helper.

---

## 3 — Methodology

### 3.1 — Detection (`detect_high_regains`)
Filter the match to the team's events that are (a) a recovery/interception/tackle, (b) at `x ≥ 66.7`, and (c) for tackles, successful (`outcome == 1`). When `open_play_only=True` (default), regains from set-piece restarts are excluded. The result is the qualifying high-regain rows with an absolute `_match_sec` column.

### 3.2 — Linking to shots (`link_regains_to_shots`)
For each high regain, find the **first shot by the same team within `WINDOW_SEC = 15 s`**. A regain with such a shot is "linked to shot"; if that shot was a goal it is also "linked to goal". The elapsed time to the shot is recorded.

### 3.3 — Possession value (`_compute_pv_for_linked`)
For linked regains, the possession-value generated by the sequence from the regain to the shot is computed via the PV model ([possession-value-model.md](../../models/possession-value-model.md)), giving `total_pv_from_regains` and `avg_pv_per_regain`.

### 3.4 — Top zones (`_compute_top_zones`)
High regains are aggregated by pitch zone to identify the most productive high-regain locations (`top_regain_zones`).

### 3.5 — KPIs (`compute_high_regain_kpis`)
Assembles counts and rates: total regains, linked-to-shot, linked-to-goal, shot/goal conversion rates, average time to shot, PV totals/averages, top zones, regain-type breakdown, and linked-shot details. This is called from within `ChanceCreationAnalyzer.analyse`, so the KPIs appear in the Chance Creation card.

### 3.6 — League table (`build_league_table`)
Aggregates high-regain KPIs across a season for all teams, supporting ranking/benchmarking.

---

## 4 — Key Metrics & Definitions

- **Total high regains:** recoveries/interceptions/successful tackles at `x ≥ 66.7` (open play).
- **Linked to shot / goal:** regains followed by a team shot / goal within 15 s.
- **Shot conversion rate:** linked-to-shot ÷ total (0–1).
- **Goal conversion rate:** linked-to-goal ÷ total (0–1).
- **Avg time to shot (s):** mean elapsed time on linked regains.
- **Total / avg PV from regains:** possession value generated by linked regain sequences.
- **Top regain zones:** highest-volume high-regain pitch zones.
- **Regain-types breakdown:** counts by recovery / interception / tackle.

---

## 5 — Outputs

- **KPI dict** (keys above) embedded in the Chance Creation result under `high_regain_kpis`.
- **Visual outputs:** high-regain zone map, conversion KPIs, PV-generated figure (within the Chance Creation card).
- **Season:** league table for benchmarking.
- **No parquet** — computed live.

---

## 6 — Methodological Decisions & Rationale

- **`x ≥ 66.7` (attacking third):** a regain must be genuinely high to count as pressing payoff; recoveries in the middle/defensive thirds are excluded.
- **Successful tackles only (`outcome == 1`):** an unsuccessful tackle did not win the ball, so it is not a regain.
- **Open-play exclusion of set pieces:** a "regain" off a set-piece restart is not the result of high pressing, so it is filtered out by default.
- **15 s regain→shot window:** ties the link to genuine transition speed; a shot far later is a separate phase, not a product of the regain.
- **PV as a productivity measure beyond shots:** even regains that don't end in a shot can advance goal probability; PV captures this so the metric isn't purely shot-binary.
- **Integration into Chance Creation:** high regains are an offensive chance-creation source, so the KPIs live alongside the origin breakdown rather than in a separate tab.

---

## 7 — Limitations & Known Issues

- **First-shot linking only:** only the first shot in the window is linked; a regain that produces a second-phase shot after 15 s is not credited.
- **Event-only regains:** pressing that forces a hurried clearance recovered elsewhere may not register as a high regain.
- **PV inherits model limitations:** see [possession-value-model.md](../../models/possession-value-model.md) (telescoping chain value, fallback grid).
- **Zone productivity is volume-based** unless paired with conversion; a high-volume zone isn't necessarily a high-conversion one.
